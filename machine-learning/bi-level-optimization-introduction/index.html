Bi level Optimization - Part 1 - Example and Basics

1. Mô tả

Cho một hàm f,g:Rn×Rn→R parameterized by θf​,θg​. Chúng ta có thể viết dưới dạng sau. f(x,y;θf​) và g(x,y;θg​)

Bài toán tối ưu hai cấp được định nghĩa như sau. Ta tìm x∗ thoả mãn

x∗=argminf(x,y∗)

Sao cho

y∗=argming(x∗,y)

2. Ví dụ

Cho hai hàm f,g:R×R→R như sau. Tìm (x∗,y∗) thoả mãn điều kiện trên

{f(x,y)=7x2.−12xy+4y2−30x+4y−2g(x,y)=4y2−4xy+x2+17​
Giải:

Ta tìm y∗ trước, cho đạo hàm ∇g=0. Với mọi x∈R, ta có

∇y∇g(x,y)​=8y−4x=0⟹y∗=2x​​

Điều này tức là giá trị của g(x,y) cực tiểu khi y∗=y=2x​ với mọi x∈R. Thế điều này vào phương trình đầu ta có

f(x,y∗)=2x2−28x−2

Cho đạo hàm theo x của f bằng 0 ta có:

4x−28=0⟹x=7⟹y=3.5

vậy (x∗,y∗)=(7,3.5) là nghiệm tối ưu của bài toán trên.

General Bi-level Optimization Problems

Given f,g:D⊂Rd×Rd→R is two function. Find (x∗,yopt∗​) satisfied the unconstraint optimization problems below:

x∗=xargmin​F(x)=f(x,y∗(x)) s.ty∗(x)=yargmin​g(x,y) ​(1.1)(1.2)​
Assumptions

We will have some assumptions for the exists of solution of the problem defined above:

Assumption 1: Lipschitz continuity

We will assume that f,∇f,∇g,∇2g are respectively ℓf,0​,ℓf,1​,ℓg,1​,ℓg,2​ - Lipschitz continuous with the input variable ∀(x1​,y1​)​∈D

Note: This is for the analysis of non-convex, this assumptions usually used in both convex and non-convex analysis

Assumption 2: Strong convexity of g in y

For any fixed x, g(x,y) is strongly convex in y with parameter μg​

Note: This assumption are used when analyzed the convergence rate in the ℓ2​-norm of the sum of the gradient matrices over iterations

Note: guarantee for unique value of y∗ with a fixed x

General solution

As usual when solving a single-level optimization problem on f, we also try to get the first-order gradient w.r.t x and set that to zero, then solving the achieved equation by different solvers. We will try to do the same here. Let ∇F(x) is a first-order gradient of f w.r.t only x. Because we will have y∗ conditioned on x, so we can consider y∗ is a function with x as an input. So when calculating the ∇F(x), we must use the total derivatives instead of partial one.

Total derivatives of nested functions

Given y:Rn→Rm and f:Rn×Rm→R. We have

dxdf(x,y(x))​=dxdf(x,y)​+dxdy​dydf(x,y)​(2.1)

Now we will try to reformulate the second part of the (2.2). Look at the (1.2), we obtain the first-order optimality condition of g(x,y) w.r.t to y we have:

∇y​g(x,y∗(x))=0(2.3)

Now take the gradient w.r.t x of two pieces, we have:

∇xy​g(x,y∗(x))+∇x​y∗(x)⊤∇yy​g(x,y∗(x))=0 ⟹∇x​y∗(x)⊤=−∇xy​g(x,y∗(x))[∇yy​g(x,y∗(x))]−1​(2.4)(2.5)​

Let (2.5) to (2.2) we have a final solution here

∇F(x)=∇x​f(x,y∗(x))+−∇xy​g(x,y∗(x))[∇yy​g(x,y∗(x))]−1∇y​f(x,y∗(x))(2.2)

Solve the equation of ∇F(x)=0 we got the x.

Limitation of general solution
  • The term ∇yy​g(x,y∗(x)) have highly computational cost when doing with the large dataset in the context of machine learning.
  • We usually do not have to deal with the closed-form function, it will be solving to find a parameter of a network/function iteratively

So we introduce a new term called Stochastic derivatives, a assumption for those Stochastic derivatives and definition for a stochastic bilevel optimization problem

Definition of stochastic function: A function f,g now, will be defined with additional random variable ξ,ϕ, respectively and denoted as f(⋅,⋅;ξ) and g(⋅,⋅;ϕ). This two variable can demonstrated any random property of f and g, here we can intuitively thinking about its will be a network parameters.

Assumption on stochastic derivatives: Gradient of f(⋅,⋅;ξ) and g(⋅,⋅;ϕ) and hessian ∇2g respectively are unbiased estimator of ∇f and ∇g and ∇2g where ξ,ϕ are drawn from a their distribution. Which mean for any fixed x,y

Eξ​[∇f(⋅,⋅;ξ)]=∇f(⋅,⋅)Eϕ​[∇g(⋅,⋅;ϕ)]=∇g(⋅,⋅)Eϕ​[∇2g(⋅,⋅;ϕ)]=∇2g(⋅,⋅)​(2.3)​

In our machine learning context, we will use the Alternating Stochastic Gradient Descent to solve the problem of stochastic nested (bi level) problem. The algorithm is inspired by naive SGD of single-level optimization problem. Because at each iteration, we required a machine-compatible computing version of the gradient on the objectives w.r.t to its variables x,y. The gradient of objective w.r.t y at the epoch k and sub-iteration t defined as hgk,t​. Similarly, the gradient of objective F w.r.t x at iteration k defined as hfk​ as below:

hgk,t​=∇y​g(xk,yk,t;ϕk,t)hfk​=∇x​f(x,y∗(x);ξk,)−∇xy​g(x,y∗(x);ϕk,t)[∇yy​g(x,y∗(x);ϕk,t]−1∇y​f(x,y∗(x);;ξk)​(2.4)(2.5)​
Hessian approximation

Due to the term [∇yy​g(x,y∗(x)]−1 is not efficiency when computing by machine, in our algorithm, we will approximate this by using , and also this help reducing the bias when estimate ∇x​f(x,y).

Estimation is:

[∇yy​g(x,y∗(x)]−1≈​ℓg,1​N​n=1∏N′​(I−ℓg,1​1​∇yy2​g(x,y;ϕ(n)​))​(2.6)

We obtained this by using Neumann expansion

Stochastic Gradient Descent for nested optimization problem
Algorithm 1: ALSET
-------------------
Initialize: x0, y0, stepsizes {a_k, b_k}
for k in 0..K-1
do
	for t in 0..T-1
	SET y_{k, 0} = y_{k}
	do
		UPDATE y_{k, t+1} := y_{k, t} - b_k * hg_{k, t}
	done - endfor
	SET y_{k + 1} = y_{k, T}
	UPDATE x_{k + 1} = x_{k} - a_k hf_{k}
done - endfor

Some note:

  • Basically, we are doing the gradient descent on finding optimal y first. After we got “a better” y, we will find a “better” x by taking the deepest step towards to inverse direction of gradient respect to x of F(x)
  • If we just find for the optimal y at each epoch, the speed will be very slow. There is a paper doing this before and saw that the results will be the same. And based on assumption two, we can guarantee the convergence of y

Analyzing

Convex analyze

The questions I raised here is: What is the term that a bi level optimization problems will be analyzed.

In some single-level convex optimization problems, when introduce new algorithms or optimizer, they usually tends to analyze the convergence rate of a function. In the other words, how fast the solution will approaching the global minimum. For example, learning function is f and the parameter is w, they will analyze the speed of ∣ω−ωopt​∣→0. Assume they have the following result:

∣ωk+1​−ωopt​∣≤σ∣ωk​−ωopt​∣

So will call the convergence rate of the algorithm is O(σ).

Non-convex analyze

Due to our assumption do not mention about convexity, we will analyze the “converge to zero rate” term of average expectation of norms of gradient matrix. Which mean

K1​k=1∑K​E[​∇F(xk)​2]

We analyze this term because this will show how fastly x converge to the solution (because at the solution, the norm will be zero and the expectations of norm will be expected to be near 0.

As also, due to this is bi-level optimization, we also have to find out the convergence rate of the solution of y defined by E[​yK−y∗(xK)​2]. This is because the y is strictly convex, so we can analyze the term following the convex rules. However, this is a “stochastic problem” so we better to analyze the “expectation” of the convergence.
